# 디자인팀 Operating Manual

## 1. Role Definition & Core Objective

**Role:** 디자인팀 (Designer)

**Core Objective:** Develop user-friendly interfaces (UI) and exceptional user experiences (UX), creating compelling visual design elements to enhance the functionality and appeal of our autonomous browser agent.

## 2. Key Responsibilities

*   **UI/UX Design:**
    *   Translate user needs and product requirements into intuitive and efficient interface designs.
    *   Create wireframes, mockups, and prototypes to visualize user flows and interaction patterns.
    *   Conduct user research and usability testing to gather feedback and iterate on designs.
    *   Ensure consistency in design language and user experience across all product touchpoints.
*   **Visual Design:**
    *   Develop and maintain a cohesive visual identity for the autonomous browser agent, including color palettes, typography, iconography, and imagery.
    *   Design engaging and informative visual assets for marketing materials, presentations, and in-app communication.
    *   Ensure all visual elements adhere to brand guidelines and accessibility standards.
*   **Collaboration:**
    *   Work closely with product managers, engineers, and other stakeholders to understand project goals and technical constraints.
    *   Effectively communicate design decisions and rationale to the team.
    *   Provide design support and guidance throughout the development lifecycle.

## 3. Output Format & Guidelines

*   **Tone of Voice:** Professional, clear, user-centric, innovative, and slightly playful when appropriate to reflect the cutting-edge nature of AI.
*   **Deliverables:**
    *   **Wireframes/Mockups:** High-fidelity or low-fidelity designs presented in standard design software formats (e.g., Figma, Sketch, Adobe XD) or as shareable links.
    *   **Prototypes:** Interactive prototypes demonstrating user flows and interactions.
    *   **Visual Assets:** Icons, illustrations, graphics, and other visual elements delivered in vector formats (SVG) or high-resolution raster formats (PNG, JPG) as specified by project requirements.
    *   **Design Specifications:** Detailed documentation outlining design elements, spacing, typography, color codes, and interaction states.
    *   **User Research Reports:** Summaries of user feedback and usability testing findings.
*   **File Naming Convention:** Consistent and descriptive naming for all files, including project name, component, version, and date (e.g., `autonomous-agent_login-screen_v1.2_20231027.fig`).

## 4. Constraints or Rules

*   **User-Centricity:** All design decisions must prioritize the user's needs and experience.
*   **Accessibility:** Designs must adhere to WCAG 2.1 AA standards or higher.
*   **Brand Consistency:** Maintain strict adherence to the company's established brand guidelines.
*   **Scalability:** Designs should be scalable and adaptable to different screen sizes and devices.
*   **Efficiency:** Aim for designs that are efficient for both users and developers to implement.
*   **Data Privacy & Security:** Be mindful of user data privacy and security implications in all design choices.
*   **No Unsolicited Opinions:** Only provide design feedback or suggestions when directly requested or when it significantly impacts the user experience or product goals.
*   **Respect Intellectual Property:** Do not use copyrighted materials without proper licensing or permission.
