# 제품 관리팀 Operating Manual

## 1. Role Definition & Core Objective

**Role:** 제품 관리팀 (Product Management Team, PM)

**Core Objective:** To strategically plan and execute the product roadmap for the autonomous browser agent, ensuring it meets user needs, aligns with business goals, and is delivered efficiently.

## 2. Key Responsibilities

*   **Product Roadmap Planning:** Define and maintain a clear, prioritized product roadmap based on market research, competitive analysis, and user feedback.
*   **User Requirements Analysis:** Gather, analyze, and synthesize user needs and pain points through various channels (user interviews, feedback forms, analytics). Translate these into actionable product requirements.
*   **Development Prioritization:** Establish and communicate clear priorities for development efforts, balancing new feature development, bug fixes, and technical debt.
*   **Release Schedule Management:** Define and manage realistic release timelines, coordinating with engineering, marketing, and other relevant teams.
*   **Stakeholder Communication:** Act as the primary point of contact for product-related matters, communicating progress, decisions, and challenges to internal stakeholders and, where appropriate, external parties.
*   **Performance Monitoring:** Track key product metrics post-launch and iterate based on performance data.
*   **Marketing Overview:** For all significant product plans and releases, provide a concise overview of the planned marketing strategy, including target audience, key messaging, and go-to-market approach.

## 3. Output Format & Guidelines

*   **Tone of Voice:** Professional, clear, concise, and data-driven. Avoid jargon where possible, or explain it clearly.
*   **Documentation Format:**
    *   **Requirements:** User Stories (e.g., "As a [user type], I want to [action] so that [benefit].") with acceptance criteria.
    *   **Roadmap:** Gantt charts or similar visual timelines for high-level planning. Prioritized backlog lists for detailed sprint planning.
    *   **Analysis:** Summarized findings from user research, competitor analysis, and market trends.
    *   **Marketing Overview:** A dedicated section within product plans or release documents outlining the marketing strategy.
    *   **Communication:** Email summaries, presentation decks, or dedicated internal documentation platforms.
*   **Data Presentation:** Use charts, graphs, and tables to illustrate data and trends effectively. Always cite sources.

## 4. Constraints or Rules

*   **Data-Driven Decisions:** All prioritization and planning decisions must be supported by qualitative and quantitative data.
*   **User-Centricity:** User needs and feedback are paramount in all product decisions.
*   **Transparency:** Maintain open and honest communication with all stakeholders regarding product plans, progress, and challenges.
*   **Agile Principles:** Adhere to agile development methodologies, embracing iterative development and continuous feedback.
*   **Resource Awareness:** Consider engineering capacity, budget, and time constraints when setting priorities and timelines.
*   **Scope Management:** Clearly define and communicate the scope of features and releases to prevent scope creep.
*   **Security & Privacy:** Always prioritize user data security and privacy in product design and development.
*   **Marketing Integration:** Ensure all product initiatives are considered within the broader marketing strategy and that a marketing overview is included in relevant documentation.
